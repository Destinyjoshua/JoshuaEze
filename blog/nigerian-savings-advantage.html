The Nigerian Savings Advantage Nobody Is Talking About

I want to talk about something that most Nigerians completely overlook. And for those of us living abroad, it might be one of the smartest conversations you have with yourself this year.

No bank in the world is going to consistently pay you 20% to leave your money with them. In the UK you’re lucky to get 5%. In the US, people spent years getting less than 1%. Your money sits there, the bank uses it, and they throw you scraps in return.

Nigeria is a different story.

Treasury Bills and why they matter

When you buy a Nigerian Treasury Bill you are lending money directly to the Federal Government. Not a company. Not a startup. The government. And in return they pay you back with interest — over 20% annually.

They have never defaulted. Not once.

I want you to sit with that for a second. A government-backed savings instrument paying over 20%. There is almost nothing like this in the Western world. And yet most Nigerians either don’t know about it or don’t bother.

You can access it from as little as ₦5,000 through partner banks and savings platforms. The barrier is almost nonexistent.

What this means if you’re abroad

If you have naira in Nigeria that you’re not planning to touch for the next 6 to 12 months — it should not be sitting in a standard savings account. That money can be earning serious returns while you’re away.

Yes, the naira has challenges. Everyone knows that. But if the money was staying in Nigeria regardless, why leave it idle? The yield advantage is too significant to ignore.

Where to actually start

You don’t need a broker. You don’t need connections. Piggyvest, Cowrywise, and FairMoney all offer savings products paying up to 18.5% annually. Ten minutes on your phone and you’re set up.

The opportunity isn’t hidden. It never was. We just weren’t taught to look at our own market this way.

Your savings should work as hard as you do. In Nigeria right now — that’s actually possible.
